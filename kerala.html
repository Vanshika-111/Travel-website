History

Foundation and Planning

Founded in 1727 by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II, the ruler of the Kachhwaha Rajput dynasty.
Jaipur was built as a new capital when Amber (the earlier capital) faced water shortages and growing population issues.
Jai Singh II was a mathematician and astronomer, which influenced his desire for a well-planned and scientific city layout.

Urban Design & Architecture

Jaipur is one of India’s first planned cities, built using grid patterns based on Vastu Shastra. (ancient Hindu architectural
science)Designed by Vidyadhar Bhattacharya, a Bengali architect and scholar of architecture, astronomy and vastu.
Divided into nine blocks (Chowkris) — two reserved for the royal palace and seven for public and commercial use.

The Pink City Legacy

In 1876, the city was painted terracotta pink (a symbol of hospitality) to welcome the Prince of Wales (later King Edward VII).
The tradition of maintaining the pink color continues, earning Jaipur the nickname "The Pink City".
The color is now mandated by law in parts of the old city.

Modern Significance

Jaipur is now a UNESCO World Heritage City (declared in 2019).
Part of the famous Golden Triangle (Delhi, Agra, Jaipur) — a must-visit for Indian and international tourists.
Famous for royal heritage, handicrafts, forts, palaces, and festivals like Jaipur Literature Festival.
