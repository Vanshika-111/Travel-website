About

Rome, the capital of Italy, is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world.Known as the "Eternal City",
it seamlessly blends ancient ruins, Renaissance art, Baroque fountains, and modern Italian culture.

History

753 BCE

Founded by Romulus, according to Roman mythology.

Roman Kingdom (753-509 BCE)

Governed by kings before transitioning to a republic.

Roman Republic (509-27 BCE)

A period of expansion, democratic governance, and military power.

Roman Empire (27 BCE-476 CE)

Led by emperors like Augustus, Nero, and Constantine the Great.

Rise of Christianity

Rome became the center of Christianity; Constantine legalized the faith.

Fall of Western Empire (476 CE)

Began the Medieval period and fragmentation of power.
