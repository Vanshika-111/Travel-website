About

New Zealand is a stunning island country in the southwestern Pacific Ocean, known for its breathtaking landscapes, rich Māori
culture, and adventure-friendly vibe. It consists mainly of two landmasses — the North Island and South Island — and numerous
smaller islands. It’s a paradise for nature lovers, thrill-seekers, and anyone seeking a peaceful escape into pristine wilderness.

History:


Māori Settlement (~1200s CE)

Polynesian explorers settled and formed Māori tribes with a deep connection to nature.


1642

Dutch explorer Abel Tasman was the first European to sight New Zealand.


1769

British explorer Captain James Cook mapped the coastline and established contact.

1850s-1900s

Grew as a settler colony; developed parliamentary democracy and infrastructure.
